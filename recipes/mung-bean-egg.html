Mung Bean Egg Recipe

This recipe for a vegan egg mixture emulates the popular 'Just Egg' product. The egg mixture uses mung beans as a base and is quick, easy and cheap.

The recipe is very versitile as you can use it to cook a vegan version of scrambled eggs or use it to create an omlette by adding your favourite. You can also add it to fried rice or stir fries just like scrambled eggs are traditionally added to those dishes.

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup Yellow Mung beans, soaked in water overnight
  • 1 Cup Plant-Based Milk
  • 1/4 Cup Nutrional Yeast
  • 1 TSP Onion Powder
  • 1 TSP Garlic Powder
  • 2 TBS Oil
  • 2 TBS Potato or Tapioca Starch
  • 1 TSP Baking Powder
  • 1 TSP Black Salt
  • Salt and Pepper to Taste

Instructions

  1. Soak mung beans overnight in water. Drain soaked mung beans and add to a high speed blender or food processor
  2. Add all remaining ingredients except for the black salt
  3. Blend on high until the mixture is completely mixed, sooth, thin and pourable.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan or add oil to a pan
  5. For scrambled eggs cook for 2 minutes and When parts of the mixture start to look like they're "drying", use a spatula to stir and move the mixture around to create a scrambled texture. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. For omlette cook until completely cook on one side and then flip over and cook for 1-2 more minutes
  7. Season with black salt, regular salt, pepper, and paprika to taste
